Hillsboro, Ohio

The idea to create a 3D printer started my senior year in high school, when a buddy of mine and I discussed quite thoroughly what it would take to build one. At the time it was just a thought, but the summer before my freshman year at The Ohio State University I began construction.

Over time more and more parts of the printer were collected and inturn added. The linear bearings were ordered from Mcmaster Carr, and the stepper motors from a seller on Amazon. It should be noted that the extruder base was made from an old Altoids Sour case. This Altoids case serves not only as a base for the extruder, but also as a heat sink so as the PLA plastic will not get to hot and thus loss its integrity.
