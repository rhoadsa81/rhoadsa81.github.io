Hillsboro, Ohio
Personal Endeavor

The idea to create a 3D printer started my senior year in high school, when a buddy of mine and I discussed quite thoroughly what it would take to build one. At the time it was just a thought, but the summer before my freshman year at The Ohio State University I began construction.

Over time more and more parts of the printer were collected and inturn added. A majority of my knowledge on the construction of the printer came from the 3D printing community located at RepRap. The linear bearings were ordered from Mcmaster Carr, and the stepper motors from a seller on Amazon. It should be noted that the extruder base was made from an old Altoids Sour case. This Altoids case serves not only as a base for the extruder, but also as a heat sink so as the PLA plastic will not get to hot and thus loss its integrity.
To control the functions of the 3D printer, a Printrboard controller was purchased. The firmware behind the master piece is Marlin which is an open source firmware package to help control delta 3D printers. Marlin is for Arduino compatible setups which the Printrboard is. The first movements of the printer can be seen below. It should be noted that a sharpie was used and a circle was attempted to be drawn. The circle itself did not turn out, in large part due to the sharpie not being perfectly parallel with the base of the printer. This was adjusted in the firmware to alleviate the problem. The first print, on the other hand, was a small callibration cube. Inital layers of the cube were whopper jawed, but over time the printer adjusted itself and fixed the issue. The only way to prevent this issue from occuring in the future is to increase the resolution of the arms holding the extruder and making sure each of these arms are the exact same length.


The arms holding the extruder where updated from wooden arms with magnetic spheres at the end of each arm, to aluminum arms with traxxas ball joints at each end of the arms. The hot end base was also updated through the use of legos and duct tape. Duct tape was used as a medium to help distribute the heat from the hot end.

Recently I have updated many parts of the printer in hopes of improving the overall print quality. I improved the hotend base by using lego's, I improved some of the supports throughout the machine and I also switched g-code software from Slic3r to Cura. These three main changes seemed to make a tremendous difference in print quality.

With a new found confidence in my printing abilities, I began to print out parts that could potentially replace current parts. I was making a Replicator of sorts for any SG-1 fans out there.
