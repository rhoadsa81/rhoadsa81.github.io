Hillsboro, Ohio

The idea to create a 3D printer started my senior year in high school, when a buddy of mine and I discussed quite thoroughly what it would take to build one. At the time it was just a thought, but the summer before my freshman year at The Ohio State University I began construction.

Over time more and more parts of the printer were collected and inturn added. A majority of my knowledge on the construction of the printer came from the 3D printing community located at RepRap. The linear bearings were ordered from Mcmaster Carr, and the stepper motors from a seller on Amazon. It should be noted that the extruder base was made from an old Altoids Sour case. This Altoids case serves not only as a base for the extruder, but also as a heat sink so as the PLA plastic will not get to hot and thus loss its integrity.
To control the functions of the 3D printer, a Printrboard controller was purchased. The firmware behind the master piece is Marlin which is an open source firmware package to help control delta 3D printers. Marlin is for Arduino compatible setups which the Printrboard is.
