Aluminum Foundary
Personal endeavor

Harnessing fire has always been a passion of mankind. Harnessing the power to change the state of metal has been one of mine. The aluminum foundry I have created reaches temperatures well over 1200 degrees fahrenheit. The container that the foundry is made out of is an old Hydrogen gas cannister. The walls are filled with a mixure of sand and plaster of paris creating an insulating layer which reflects the temperatures of the foundry back in on itself.
As it currently stands I have melted down just under 5 pounds of aluminum. My main source of aluminum has been pop cans. This source is not pure aluminum, but rather an alloy of magnesium and aluminum (the tops of aluminum cans consist of both aluminum and mangnesium for magnesium's strenth)

Every part of the foundry, excluding the plaster of paris, was found around the farm. This includes the hairdryer to the welded crucible seen below. It should be noted that the crucible is made from steel.


Using the 3D printer in tandem with my foundry, I have started to learn the art of casting. The method I have implemented is called "Green Sand" casting. The first step in casting is to make "Green Sand". This is done by mixing two parts sand and one part bentonite clay. A slight amount of Water is then applied to this mixture so as the sand will stay together when compressed.
